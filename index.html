Kunjithai Sree Bala Subrahmanya Swamy Temple, popularly known as Kunjithai Kshethram, is a renowned Hindu temple situated in Kunjithai village near North Paravoor in the Ernakulam district of Kerala. Nestled gracefully on the banks of the sacred Periyar River, the temple stands as an important spiritual center for the surrounding coastal and fishing communities, who traditionally depend on fishing for their livelihood.

The temple is dedicated to Lord Bala Subrahmanya (Palani Murugan), the youthful form of Lord Murugan, symbolizing strength, wisdom, and divine protection. Devotees from nearby regions visit the temple seeking blessings for health, prosperity, and success.

Kunjithai Kshethram is administered by Sree Chithira Vaala Samajam, which oversees the temple’s rituals, festivals, and social activities. The serene riverside setting, combined with its spiritual atmosphere, makes the temple a place of peace and devotion. The temple is especially known for its annual festivals and special poojas, during which the temple becomes a vibrant center of cultural and religious life in the region.
